TERMINOLOGY

Gay: someone who likes people of the same gender. It can also be used as an umbrella term for members of the LGBTQ+ community.

Lesbian: a non-man who likes non-men. Historically, it had been used to describe women who like women, but more recently has been expanded to include non-binary people.

Bisexual: someone who likes two genders. It can also be used as an umbrella term for people who like more than one gender.

Pansexual: someone who likes people of all genders. Some pansexuals are genderblind, meaning that gender doesn’t play as a factor in their attraction to someone.

Asexual: Someone who doesn’t experiance sexual attraction. This can also be an umbrella term for those who experience little to no sexual attraction.

Demisexual: Someone who only experiences sexual attraction after an emotional bond has been established

Transgender: someone who’s gender identity is different than the one they were assigned at birth. This includes non-binary people, but most of the time is referred to people who switch between man and woman. For example, if Emily is a trans girl, it means that she is a girl, who’s assigned sex at birth was male, and then she transitioned.

Non-binary: Someone who identifies as something besides a girl or a boy.

Agender: Someone who doesn’t have a gender

Genderfluid: Someone who’s gender changes. Sometimes they may feel like a girl, others a boy, and sometimes neither or both.

Queer: Someone who is a member of the LGBTQ+ community. Someone who is genderqueer is someone who isn’t cis.

Cisgender: Someone whose gender identity matches their assigned gender at birth.

Straight: Someone who likes people of the “opposite” gender.” Also called heterosexual

Of course, these are countless other labels that people identify with, these are just some umbrella terms! Check out our resources page if you want to learn more.
